River and Flume Rafting

The Kings River

The Kings River originates in the southern Sierra Nevada Mountains as snowmelt. It is a National Wild and Scenic River with many offshoots. The river flows for 132.9 miles and moves on average 2,287 cubic feet of water a second! The Kings River is considered a Class 3 river, meaning that it is great for beginner and intermediate rafters. Because the river comes from snowmelt, the depth varies seasonally and year to year. In order to have the best experience, we recommend visting from late April to mid July, so you get the water before it evaporates.

Logging and Flumes

When the giant Sequoia trees were first discovered many people thought that they would be perfect for logging. The logging of these beautiful trees began in 1862 and got more popular from then on until the early 1900s. To speed up the transportation process, the logging companies built flumes or log chutes to take the logs down the mountain to the waiting railroads. The flumes had another use occationally, as an ambulance of sorts. If a man was injured up on the mountain, the workers would hollow out a log and send that man down the flumes in it. It was the fastest way to get help.
